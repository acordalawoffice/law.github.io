        <h3 style="font-size: 2.0rem; color: #1a202c; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 10px;">AWOL is Not Abandonment: The Legal Steps to Terminate an Absent Employee</h3>
        <div class="article-meta" style="font-size: 0.85rem; color: #718096; margin-bottom: 20px;">Published: July 2026 | Category: Dispute Avoidance</div>

        <div class="article-body" style="color: #2d3748; line-height: 1.7; text-align: justify;">
            <p style="margin-bottom: 20px;">
                Many business owners assume that if an employee goes "Absent Without Leave" (AWOL) for a week, they have resigned. This is a dangerous legal misconception. AWOL is merely a disciplinary infraction; <strong>Job Abandonment</strong> is a deliberate legal ground for termination that must be explicitly proven.
            </p>

            <h4 style="font-size: 1.15rem; color: #2d3748; margin-top: 25px; margin-bottom: 12px;">The Two-Prong Test for Abandonment:</h4>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 15px;">To successfully defend a termination based on job abandonment, the employer must establish:</p>
            <ol style="padding-left: 24px; margin-bottom: 20px;">
                <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;">The employee failed to report for work without a valid, justifiable reason.</li>
                <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;">The employee clearly intended to sever the employer-employee relationship (deliberate intent).</li>
            </ol>

            <h4 style="font-size: 1.15rem; color: #2d3748; margin-top: 25px; margin-bottom: 12px;">The Mandated Administrative Process:</h4>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 0;">
                Do not clear out their desk or stop their payroll processing immediately. You must send a <strong>Notice to Return to Work</strong> to their last known home address via registered mail. If they fail to reply or report back within the deadline specified in your notice, you must then issue a standard Notice to Explain for their unexcused absences before finalizing the termination.
            </p>
