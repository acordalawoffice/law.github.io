    <h3 style="font-size: 2.0rem; margin-top: 0; line-height: 1.3;">Demystifying Statutory Retirement Pay for Daily and Monthly Paid Employees</h3>
    <div class="article-meta" style="color: #718096; font-size: 0.9rem; margin-bottom: 15px; line-height: 1.4;">Published: July 2026 | Category: Labor Standards</div>

    <p style="text-align: justify; line-height: 1.75; margin-bottom: 22px;">
        Article 302 of the Labor Code ensures that employees who devote years of service to an enterprise are protected during their golden years. In the absence of a retirement plan or Collective Bargaining Agreement (CBA) in the workplace, an employee is entitled to statutory retirement pay upon reaching the age of <strong>sixty (60) years (optional retirement)</strong> or <strong>sixty-five (65) years (compulsory retirement)</strong>, provided they have rendered at least <strong>five (5) years of continuous service</strong> to the enterprise.
    </p>

    <h4 style="font-size: 1.3rem; line-height: 1.3; margin-top: 25px; margin-bottom: 12px;">The Legal Definition of a "Half-Month" Salary</h4>
    <p style="text-align: justify; line-height: 1.75; margin-bottom: 14px;">
        The statutory retirement pay formula dictates that a retiring employee must receive a minimum of <strong>one-half (1/2) month salary for every year of service</strong>, where a fraction of at least six (6) months is treated as a full year. However, the Supreme Court has clarified that a statutory "half-month salary" actually consists of <strong>22.5 days</strong> of pay because it must mathematically include the following components:
    </p>
    <div style="background: #f8fafc; padding: 15px; border-radius: 4px; font-size: 0.95rem; color: #1e293b; margin: 18px 0; border: 1px dashed #cbd5e1; font-weight: 600; text-align: center; line-height: 1.6;">
        Statutory Half-Month Package = 15 Days (Basic Salary) + 5 Days (Service Incentive Leave) + 2.5 Days (1/12 of 13th-Month Pay)
    </div>

    <h4 style="font-size: 1.3rem; line-height: 1.3; margin-top: 25px; margin-bottom: 12px;">Computation Methodologies</h4>
    <p style="text-align: justify; line-height: 1.75; margin-bottom: 14px;">
        The mathematical calculation varies based on how the employee's pay structure is set up:
    </p>
    <ol style="padding-left: 20px; margin-bottom: 22px; color: #333; text-align: justify; line-height: 1.75;">
        <li style="margin-bottom: 14px;">
            <strong>For Daily-Paid Employees:</strong> Multiply the worker's latest Daily Minimum Wage (DWR) by 22.5 days, then multiply that product by their total years of service.
            <br><span style="font-family: monospace; font-size: 0.95rem; color: #b45309; background-color: #fffbeb; padding: 4px 8px; border-radius: 3px; display: inline-block; margin-top: 6px; line-height: 1.4;">Formula: Retirement Pay = (Latest DWR × 22.5) × Total Years of Service</span>
        </li>
        <li style="margin-bottom: 14px;">
            <strong>For Monthly-Paid Employees:</strong> First, derive the equivalent daily rate by taking the Monthly Basic Salary, multiplying it by 12, and dividing it by the total applicable working days in a year (e.g., 313 days or 365 days depending on the category). Once the daily rate is established, apply the 22.5-day baseline multiplier.
            <br><span style="font-family: monospace; font-size: 0.95rem; color: #b45309; background-color: #fffbeb; padding: 4px 8px; border-radius: 3px; display: inline-block; margin-top: 6px; line-height: 1.4;">Formula: Retirement Pay = (Derived Daily Rate × 22.5) × Total Years of Service</span>
        </li>
    </ol>

    <blockquote style="margin: 20px 0; padding: 15px 20px; background: #fdfaf2; border-left: 4px solid #D4AF37; color: #444; text-align: justify; line-height: 1.75;">
        <strong>The Retail Exclusion:</strong> Retail, service, and agricultural establishments regularly employing <strong>fewer than ten (10) workers</strong> are explicitly exempted from the mandatory coverage of the Retirement Pay Law.
    </blockquote>
