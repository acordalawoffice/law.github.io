        <h3 style="font-size: 2.0rem; color: #1a202c; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 10px;">Navigating Due Process: The Two-Notice Rule in Philippine Labor Law</h3>
        <div class="article-meta" style="font-size: 0.85rem; color: #718096; margin-bottom: 20px;">Published by Office of Atty. Rodel P. Acorda • Legal Educational Article</div>
        
        <div class="article-body" style="color: #2d3748; line-height: 1.7; text-align: justify;">
            <p style="margin-bottom: 15px;">In the Philippines, security of tenure is a constitutionally protected right. For an employer to legally dismiss an employee, two distinct requirements must be met: substantive due process (a valid, legal reason for termination) and procedural due process (the correct legal method). A critical component of procedural due process is the "Two-Notice Rule."</p>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 20px;">Failure to comply with this rule can result in the dismissal being declared illegal, or subject the employer to significant nominal damages, even if a valid reason for termination exists.</p>
            
            <h4 style="font-size: 1.15rem; color: #2d3748; margin-top: 25px; margin-bottom: 12px;">The First Notice: Notice to Explain (NTE)</h4>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 15px;">The process begins with the first written notice, commonly referred to as the Notice to Explain. This document must be served directly to the employee and must contain specific elements:</p>
            <ul style="padding-left: 24px; margin-bottom: 20px;">
                <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;"><strong>Detailed Grounds:</strong> A clear statement of the specific causes or grounds for termination.</li>
                <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;"><strong>Detailed Narrative:</strong> A description of the specific acts or omissions complained of, including the dates and times of occurrence.</li>
                <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;"><strong>Company Policy References:</strong> A citation of the specific company rules or Labor Code provisions violated.</li>
                <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;"><strong>Opportunity to Answer:</strong> A directive giving the employee a reasonable period to submit a written explanation, defined as at least five (5) calendar days.</li>
            </ul>

            <h4 style="font-size: 1.15rem; color: #2d3748; margin-top: 25px; margin-bottom: 12px;">The Hearing or Conference</h4>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 20px;">After receiving the employee's written explanation, the employer must conduct a hearing or conference. This stage provides the employee with an opportunity to clarify their defenses, present supporting evidence, and confront any witnesses against them. The employee has the right to be assisted by counsel or a union representative.</p>

            <h4 style="font-size: 1.15rem; color: #2d3748; margin-top: 25px; margin-bottom: 12px;">The Second Notice: Notice of Decision</h4>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 15px;">If, after evaluating the evidence, the employer finds that termination is justified, a second written notice must be served—the Notice of Decision, which must outline:</p>
            <ul style="padding-left: 24px; margin-bottom: 0;">
                <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;">A clear showing that the employer considered all facts and evidence presented by the employee in their defense.</li>
                <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;">A formal statement indicating whether the employee is being terminated, suspended, or cleared.</li>
                <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;">The specific grounds mandated to justify the final penalty.</li>
            </ul>
