        <h3 style="font-size: 2.0rem; color: #1a202c; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 10px;">The 180-Day Trap: Managing Probationary Employment Without Automatic Regularization</h3>
        <div class="article-meta" style="font-size: 0.85rem; color: #718096; margin-bottom: 20px;">Published: July 2026 | Category: HR Compliance</div>

        <div class="article-body" style="color: #2d3748; line-height: 1.7; text-align: justify;">
            <p style="margin-bottom: 20px;">
                Article 296 of the Labor Code mandates that probationary employment must not exceed <strong>six (6) months</strong>. If an employee is allowed to log into work on day 181 without a clear separation notice, they automatically achieve regular employment status by operation of law.
            </p>

            <h4 style="font-size: 1.15rem; color: #2d3748; margin-top: 25px; margin-bottom: 12px;">Crucial Steps for HR Compliance:</h4>
            <ul style="padding-left: 24px; margin-bottom: 20px;">
                <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;"><strong>Communicate Standards on Day One:</strong> To successfully terminate an unsatisfactory probationary employee, you must prove that the precise performance standards were made known to them <em>at the time of engagement</em>.</li>
                <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;"><strong>The Extension Myth:</strong> You cannot casually extend a probationary period past 6 months unless there is a formal, written agreement voluntarily signed by both parties, strictly tied to an ongoing training program.</li>
                <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;"><strong>Mid-Term Evaluation:</strong> Conduct a formal performance review at Month 3 and Month 5 to ensure you have written tracking data before the final decision is implemented.</li>
            </ul>

            <blockquote style="margin: 25px 0; padding: 15px 20px; background: #fdfaf2; border-left: 4px solid #D4AF37; font-style: italic; color: #444;">
                <strong>Compliance Check:</strong> Ensure your standard onboarding documents include a signed "Acknowledgement of Performance Standards" sheet on the employee's very first day.
            </blockquote>
